Looking after you

IVF asks a lot of you — emotionally and physically. Here are gentle ways to care for yourself along the way.

However you're feeling is okay — even the hard days. Be kind to yourself; you're doing your best.

Your feelings are valid

The highs and lows are normal. Be gentle with yourself. If it feels heavy, a fertility counsellor can really help — just ask your team.

Finding calm

When it builds up, breathe in for 4 and out for 6, for a minute. A little daily mindfulness — or a distraction you love, like baking or a good book — helps too.

Gentle movement

A 20-minute walk most days lifts your mood and clears your head. Anything more strenuous, check with your team first.

Nourish & rest

There's no perfect diet — Mediterranean-style eating (vegetables, olive oil, fish, whole grains) is a gentle choice. And protect your sleep: wind down with calm music, not your phone.

You're not alone

Lean on people you trust. Set 'fertility-free' times where treatment talk is off-limits, and consider a support group of people who understand.

The two-week wait

Often the hardest part. Symptoms — or none — don't reliably predict the result. Try writing one worry and one kind thought each day; it helps the storm settle.
“I am allowed to feel every emotion. I am doing my best — and that's enough.”
If you feel overwhelmed, anxious or low for more than a few days, please tell your care team — they're here for the emotional side too, not just the medical. This app isn't for emergencies; if you're in crisis, contact your local emergency services.

About this app

An honest note on what this is — and isn't — today.

This is a demo. Everything you see is made-up sample information — not real patient data. It's here so we can show how the app will feel before it's connected to anything real.
What it does
A calm place to follow your IVF journey: your appointments, the results your clinician releases, your treatment plan, your care team, messages from the clinic, signed consents, plain-language guides, and how-to instructions for your medicines.
What it doesn't do
  • It never decides anything medical — it only shows what your clinic shares.
  • It never works out your dose or timing — that comes from your doctor.
  • It's not for emergencies, and not a replacement for talking to your team.
